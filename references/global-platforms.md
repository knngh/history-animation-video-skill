# Global Platforms

Use this reference when recommending overseas publishing platforms for historical AI animation videos. Verify official requirements when exact limits, monetization, or AI labels matter.

## Recommended Stack

Primary:

- YouTube Shorts: best long-term search, channel equity, playlists, and monetization path. Good for bilingual or English-dubbed history series.
- TikTok: best trend velocity and discovery testing. Good for fast hooks, comments, and topic validation.
- Instagram Reels: best for polished visuals, creator identity, and repackaging into carousel/story posts.

Secondary:

- Facebook Reels: useful for older audiences, history nostalgia, and English-speaking broad reach.
- Snapchat Spotlight: useful for younger audiences and rapid visual-first experiments.
- Pinterest: useful for evergreen discovery, covers, infographics, and "history aesthetic" traffic.
- X: useful for clips plus threads; not the main video host, but good for debate and backlinks.
- Reddit: use only community-native edits and factual framing; avoid spam reposting.

## Platform Fit

YouTube Shorts:

- Publish 45-75 second vertical shorts first; use 90-180 seconds only for stronger stories.
- Add searchable titles and descriptions.
- Build playlists by dynasty, figure type, and series.
- Best formats: "The Cost of Victory", "Last Night", "One Decision".

TikTok:

- Publish 35-60 seconds.
- Make the first frame readable with no context.
- Prioritize comment prompts, stitches, and quick series.
- Best formats: debate, reversal, "you learned this wrong".

Instagram Reels:

- Publish 30-60 seconds with polished covers.
- Make thumbnail and profile grid coherent.
- Repackage as carousel: first slide = cover, next slides = facts vs drama.
- Best formats: cinematic visuals, quote cards, women in history, culture aesthetics.

Facebook Reels:

- Publish 45-90 seconds.
- Use clearer narration and less slang.
- Best formats: nostalgia, familiar figures, moral dilemma, family-shareable stories.

Snapchat Spotlight:

- Publish 15-45 seconds.
- Use fast visual changes and simple captions.
- Best formats: object hook, transformation, "before/after history".

Pinterest:

- Publish vertical video pins and static covers.
- Treat content as searchable evergreen assets.
- Best formats: visual timelines, costume/setting references, quote cards, "saveable" history facts.

X:

- Publish 30-90 second clips with a short thread.
- Lead with the strongest claim, then add sources or caveats below.
- Best formats: debate, contrarian interpretation, source screenshots, production behind-the-scenes.

Reddit:

- Do not mass post.
- Adapt each post to the subreddit rules.
- Lead with historical discussion, not channel promotion.
- Best formats: source-grounded clips, "I animated this moment, what did I miss?"

## Language Strategy

- Start with Chinese if the creator is fastest in Chinese.
- For overseas growth, add English subtitles from day one.
- Test English voiceover after 10 Chinese-subtitled samples.
- Avoid literal translation. Rewrite hooks for the target culture.

English hook examples:

- "History remembers his victory. It forgot the price."
- "This was not bravery. It was a point of no return."
- "One letter changed the rest of his life."
- "If you were him, would you make the same choice?"

## Cross-Post Rules

- Never upload one identical package everywhere.
- Keep the same core footage, but change hook, title, cover, caption, and ending question.
- Remove platform watermarks before cross-posting.
- Keep a spreadsheet of platform, title, first line, cover text, duration, posted time, views, retention, comments, saves, follows.

## Current Source Notes

- YouTube Shorts supports square or vertical videos up to three minutes and uses engaged views for eligibility/revenue analytics.
- TikTok supports AI-generated content labels and requires labeling realistic AI-generated content.
- TikTok ad specs list vertical 9:16 as recommended and allow up to 10 minutes for non-Spark in-feed ads, but organic short-video strategy should stay much shorter.
- Instagram Reels and Facebook Reels rules change often; verify in-app or official Meta docs before relying on exact length limits.
